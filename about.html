About Me

My Academic Journey, Research Motivation, and Future Goals

My Academic Journey

My academic journey began with a strong foundation in mathematics and science during my early education. Upon entering Sylhet Engineering College for my undergraduate studies in Computer Science and Engineering, I discovered a passion for algorithmic thinking and problem-solving through competitive programming.

During my first two years, I actively participated in programming contests, which honed my analytical skills and taught me to approach complex problems methodically. This experience proved invaluable as I transitioned to more advanced coursework in data structures, algorithms, and artificial intelligence.

As I progressed through my undergraduate studies, maintaining a CGPA of 3.96/4.00 required consistent dedication and a deep understanding of fundamental concepts across all computer science disciplines. This academic rigor laid the groundwork for my research pursuits.

From Competitive Programming to Research

My academic interests initially developed through competitive programming, which strengthened my problem-solving skills and algorithmic thinking. As I became increasingly interested in research-oriented work, I transitioned to Python, recognizing its importance for experimentation in machine learning. During this phase, I focused on data preprocessing and visualization techniques, which helped me better understand data behavior and patterns before moving into core machine learning and deep learning methodologies.

My first formal research opportunity came through collaboration with senior students, under the guidance of faculty members including the head of our department. This early work focused on handwritten Hangul alphabet recognition and introduced me to collaborative research environments, experimental design, and practical problem-solving in computer vision.

Building on this foundation, I later worked on multiple research projects spanning handwritten script recognition, handwritten medical prescription analysis, air quality index prediction using explainable AI, and other applied deep learning problems. These experiences enabled me to work across different datasets, modeling approaches, and evaluation strategies.

Research Motivation & Philosophy

My research motivation stems from a fundamental belief in technology's potential to address practical challenges, particularly in the context of Bangladesh and South Asia. I'm particularly interested in problems that combine technical complexity with meaningful societal impact.

For instance, my work on "Identifying Dominant Pollutants in Major Cities of Bangladesh Through XAI" emerged from observing the urgent need for data-driven environmental policy. Similarly, my ongoing thesis on "Vehicle & License Plate Detection Under Adverse Conditions" addresses a practical transportation challenge relevant to developing nations.

I approach research with a dual focus: methodological rigor and practical applicability. Each project begins with thorough literature review to understand the current landscape, followed by careful experimental design, and concludes with critical analysis of results. This systematic approach ensures that my work contributes meaningfully to the field while remaining grounded in real-world constraints.

Research Areas & Interests

My primary research interests span several interconnected areas within artificial intelligence and computer science:

Computer Vision

Specializing in image classification, object detection, and document analysis with applications in cultural preservation, agriculture, and transportation.

Machine Learning

Focusing on explainable AI (XAI), transfer learning, and developing models that perform well with limited labeled data—common in real-world applications.

Natural Language Processing

Exploring low-resource language processing, particularly for Bengali and related scripts, including handwritten text recognition.

Medical AI Applications

Applying computer vision and machine learning to healthcare challenges such as medical imaging analysis and disease detection.

Academic Mindset & Approach

I believe that impactful research requires both technical expertise and interdisciplinary perspective. My approach combines:

  • Rigorous methodology: Ensuring experiments are well-designed, reproducible, and thoroughly documented
  • Collaborative spirit: Actively seeking diverse perspectives through teamwork and mentorship
  • Ethical consideration: Considering the societal implications of technological developments
  • Continuous learning: Staying current with rapidly evolving fields while maintaining foundational knowledge

This mindset extends beyond research to all academic pursuits, from coursework to leadership roles. As General Secretary of student organizations, I've applied these principles to foster collaborative environments where peers can develop both technical and soft skills.

Future Research Goals & Academic Aspirations

Looking forward, I aim to pursue graduate studies that will allow me to deepen my expertise while contributing to meaningful research initiatives. My specific goals include:

Short-term (1-2 years)

Complete my ongoing research projects and undergraduate thesis, while actively seeking machine learning–focused research internships to gain deeper practical exposure. Through these experiences, I aim to strengthen my understanding of real-world ML pipelines, experimentation practices, and applied problem solving.

Medium-term (Graduate Studies)

Pursue graduate studies (Master’s or PhD) with a focus on computer vision and machine learning, particularly in low-resource and challenging environments. I am interested in developing robust and generalizable models that perform effectively under data scarcity, environmental noise, and real-world constraints.

Long-term (Career)

Build a research-oriented career that bridges academia and industry, potentially within a research university or an industrial research lab. I also aspire to mentor and collaborate with emerging researchers, contributing to the growth of the research ecosystem in Bangladesh through knowledge sharing and academic engagement.

I am particularly interested in research that addresses the unique challenges of developing nations, where technological solutions must account for resource constraints, linguistic diversity, and specific local needs. My background provides me with both the technical skills and contextual understanding necessary for this work.

Beyond Academics

Beyond formal coursework and research, I have remained actively engaged in academic and extracurricular leadership. I currently serve as the Secretary General of the CSE Society and Vice President of the CSE Research Club at Sylhet Engineering College, along with responsibilities as an Organizing Secretary in the SEC Programming Club. These roles have strengthened my skills in coordination, academic event organization, teamwork, and professional communication-qualities that are essential for collaborative research environments.

Alongside leadership activities, photography is a personal interest that complements my academic mindset. It has refined my attention to detail, patience, and observational skills, which often translate into how I analyze data, interpret visual patterns, and evaluate experimental results. Balancing research, leadership, and creative pursuits has shaped me into a focused yet adaptable individual, well prepared for the intellectual and collaborative demands of graduate study and research-driven work.
